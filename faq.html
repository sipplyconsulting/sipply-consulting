Content & AI

My no‑AI stance explained

Enough with the AI slop flooding the internet. "Elevate", "in today's landscape", "synergize", this language is an insult to trained journalistic work and to your readers. Search engines are already penalising it. More importantly, your audience can feel it. Human writing builds trust; AI filler erodes it quietly until you notice the drop in conversions.

Yes. A content audit is a standard deliverable in the strategy phase. Beyond detection tools (AI tools analyzing AI content are obviously imperfect), I read for tone, specificity, and the structural patterns AI reliably produces,vague openers, hedge-heavy conclusions, hollow bullet lists. You'll get a clear picture of what works, what reads as machine-generated, and what needs rewriting.

B2B technology, manufacturing, professional services, and consumer brands,primarily companies where credibility matters. I've worked across SaaS, industrial sectors, retail, and media. What matters more than industry is whether you take your content seriously. I don't do work for companies that want volume over quality.
