01 — Environmental cost

Every AI prompt has it's own carbon footprint.

Whenever you're asking ChatGPT to write a blog post or come up with a catchy LinkedIn post (it won't, because they all sound the same), there's more often than not a power plant burning fuel, an electricity grid straining under load, and actual water disappearing from rivers. There's no way around this, as it's happening right now, to feed machines that answer questions a normal human could think of, write, and publish.

03 — Reversal in the workforce?

Companies are rehiring people they replaced.

The rush to replace people with AI is colliding with reality. Quality drops, customers revolt, and companies are quietly walking it back.

Klarna

Replaced roughly 700 customer service employees with AI. Service quality declined, customers revolted, and the company had to rehire humans.10

29% and rising

Share of companies that made AI-related layoffs and have already rehired staff back, according to Forbes reporting on 2026 workforce data.11

Only 9%

of executives, managers and workers surveyed said they'd want to replace their entire workforce with AI tools.11
