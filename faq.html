Process

How projects actually work

Discovery means understanding your brand, audience, competitors, and goals before a single deliverable is written. That includes a structured intake brief, a competitor content audit, and a positioning session. Deep research precedes every recommendation — no guesswork, no generic strategy pulled from a template.

It depends on scope. A focused strategy engagement runs 3–4 weeks. Website copywriting for a standard 6–8 page site typically takes 2–3 weeks once the brief is complete. Ongoing retainers are scoped per cycle. You get a realistic timeline upfront and I stick to it.

Every project includes two structured feedback rounds. In practice most work lands on the first pass because the discovery phase does its job. Scope changes or new directions beyond the original brief are scoped separately.

That is common, and it is exactly why the consultation and quote builder exist. You do not need to show up with a perfectly defined scope. We can work backward from the problem, look at what is blocking growth, and figure out whether you need strategy, copy, search support, or a combination.

Content & AI

The no‑AI stance, explained

Enough with the AI slop flooding the internet. "Elevate", "in today's landscape", "synergize", this language is an insult to trained journalistic work and to your readers. Search engines are already penalising it. More importantly, your audience can feel it. Human writing builds trust; AI filler erodes it quietly until you notice the drop in conversions.

Yes. A content audit is a standard deliverable in the strategy phase. Beyond detection tools (which are imperfect), I read for tone, specificity, and the structural patterns AI reliably produces — vague openers, hedge-heavy conclusions, hollow bullet lists. You'll get a clear picture of what works, what reads as machine-generated, and what needs rewriting.

B2B technology, manufacturing, professional services, and consumer brands — primarily companies where credibility matters. I've worked across SaaS, industrial sectors, retail, and media. What matters more than industry is whether you take your content seriously. I don't do work for companies that want volume over quality.
